UK Employer Training Guide · 2025–26

You've got a skills gap.
Let's fix that.

Whether someone on your team needs to get better at their job, you're bringing in new talent, or you just know something's missing — there's a training route out there for you. The tricky bit is knowing which one.

📋
The UK has more funded training options than most employers realise
Apprenticeships. Skills Bootcamps. Free Level 3 courses. Government-funded work academies. The problem isn't a lack of options — it's knowing which ones apply to a business like yours, and which will actually get results without eating up your time or budget.
💷
A lot of it is free — especially for smaller businesses
If you employ fewer than 250 people, the government picks up most or all of the training cost for several routes. Apprenticeships for under-25s are fully funded for SMEs. Skills Bootcamps are free when you're hiring. Free Level 3 courses cost your business nothing at all. You don't need an HR department to access this — you just need to know where to look.
📈
Training pays off — for your business and your people
Businesses that invest in staff development see stronger retention, better productivity, and easier recruitment. Staff who feel invested in are less likely to leave. And with the right route, you can train someone exactly to the way your business works — not just to a generic job description.
